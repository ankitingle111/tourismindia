Torna Fort

Torna Fort, also known as Prachandagad, is a large hill fort located in Pune district, Maharashtra, India. It is historically significant as it was the first fort captured by Shivaji Maharaj in 1646, at the age of 16, forming the nucleus of the Maratha empire. Torna Fort offers breathtaking views of the Sahyadri mountain range and the surrounding valleys.

Key Features:

  • Located approximately 50 km from Pune city.
  • Elevation: 1,403 meters (4,603 feet) above sea level.
  • Popular trekking destination.
  • Fort ruins and bastions.
  • Scenic beauty and panoramic views.
  • Rich historical significance.

How to Reach:

Torna Fort can be reached by road from Pune city. The nearest railway station is Pune Junction, and the nearest airport is Pune International Airport.

Best Time to Visit:

The best time to visit Torna Fort is during the winter months (October to February) when the weather is pleasant for trekking and sightseeing.

Accommodation:

There are limited accommodation options available near Torna Fort. Visitors can opt for homestays or camping arrangements.
