Kashi Vishvanath

Kashi Vishvanath is one of the most sacred temples in Hinduism and is located in the holy city of Varanasi, Uttar Pradesh, India. It is dedicated to Lord Shiva, the deity of destruction and creation.

Location

The Kashi Vishvanath Temple is situated on the western bank of the sacred river Ganges, in the heart of Varanasi, also known as Kashi.

Significance

The temple holds immense religious significance for Hindus and is considered one of the twelve Jyotirlingas, believed to be the most sacred abodes of Lord Shiva.

Architecture

The current temple structure was built by Maharaja Ranjit Singh in the 19th century after the original temple was destroyed several times throughout history. The temple features intricate carvings and a grand spire.

Pilgrimage

Millions of devotees from all over the world visit Kashi Vishvanath every year to seek blessings from Lord Shiva. It is believed that a visit to the temple and a dip in the Ganges river washes away sins
