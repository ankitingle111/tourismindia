Kerala

Kerala, often referred to as "God's Own Country," is a state located in the southwestern part of India. It is renowned for its scenic beauty, diverse culture, and rich heritage.

Geography

Kerala is bordered by the Western Ghats on one side and the Arabian Sea on the other, resulting in a diverse landscape of lush green hills, serene backwaters, and pristine beaches.

Culture

The culture of Kerala is a blend of indigenous traditions and influences from various civilizations. It is known for its vibrant festivals, classical dance forms like Kathakali and Mohiniyattam, and delicious cuisine.

Tourist Attractions

Kerala offers a wide range of tourist attractions, including:

  • Backwaters of Alleppey
  • Munnar Hill Station
  • Periyar Wildlife Sanctuary
  • Kerala Backwaters
  • Kovalam Beach

Economy

Kerala's economy is primarily based on agriculture, tourism, and remittances from the large expatriate population. It is known for its high human development index and literacy rate.
